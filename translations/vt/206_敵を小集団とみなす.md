---
title: "Viewing Opponents as Small Groups"
---

![image](https://gyazo.com/1a060339edfa76bb39da7fbe5e0640fe/thumb/1000)
- People with [[Opinions Different from Ours]] are considered a [[Minority]] within our [[Observation Range]], leading us to believe they are also a minority in the world.
- Whether the opponent's opinion is a minority or not is unrelated to the correctness of the opinion. However, due to the [[Bias of Believing the Majority is Correct]], a misperception arises that "our correctness is validated."
