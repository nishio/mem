---
title: "The Problem of Observation Range"
---

- Humans can only [[observe]] a part of the world
- They make judgments about the unobserved based on [[generalization]] from what is observed
- This generalization is often mistaken

Related
- [[Blind Men and the Elephant]]

![image](https://gyazo.com/75dcb72c20b56830d0267946276bc88f/thumb/1000)

- We can only observe a part of the world
- The range of what can be observed may be biased
- This bias can sometimes be attributed to the observer's characteristics
    - Such as being too foolish to understand
    - Or being avoided due to usual behavior and speech
